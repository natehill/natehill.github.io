Sculpture + P5JS inspiration > Monument | Mitosis-ish / liverwort

This sketch at the bottom of the page came from tweaking the phyllotaxis algorithm as well as some code another classmate wrote. This was where I first figured out that sin() and cos() can produce these cool pulsing animated effects. Very fun.

This scultpure is wood, glue, sawdust, and paint. While we were working with the phyllotaxis algorithm, I came across a stash of bowl blanks in my basement. When making wooden bowls on a lathe, you typically turn a green piece of wood once, let it dry, then turn it again so it is ultimately perfectly round rather than warped. I took a couple of these bowl blanks, segmented them based on some of what I'd been seeing making phyllotaxis sketches, then used a jigsaw and a grinder to cut and carve them. The piece joining the two carved components is a recycled table leg.

The form of the scultpure (and the sketch) is drawn from a liverwort antheridium, with a little love for Acetabularia, an amazingly large single-celled organism.
