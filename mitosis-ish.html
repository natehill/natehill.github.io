Antheridium, 2022

This is ajavascript animation that I made using the phyllotxus algorithm in a class called Algorithmic Botany at the School for Poetic Computation. I experimented with and adjusted the code until it produced this mesmerizing, pulsing, undulating form that gave the appearance of cells dividing, expansion and contraction, a feeling of repetions of birth, life, and death.

In my workshop, I had a series of unfinished bowl blanks that I intended to turn again on my lathe after the wood had dried and stabilized. I worked on these pieces as I developed the animation; instead of making salad bowls I reshaped the pieces, carved them, and captured the spirit of the animation in their final forms. For a finish, I used many layers of sprayed, thinned out wood glue and the sawdust that I created in the carving process. Returning the removed material back to the form as a finishing coat repeated and completed the cyclical nature of the animation.

The form of the scultpure (and the sketch) is drawn from a liverwort antheridium, with a little love for Acetabularia, an amazingly large single-celled organism.
