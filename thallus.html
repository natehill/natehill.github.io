Thallus, 2023

Thallus is made from the white ash tree, a tree that has almost completely disappeared from our region due to the presence of the invasive emerlad ash borer. I sourced the wood from a local farm and sawmill and conjured a fictional future-being from the bones of a dying species.

Their form is drawn from the morphology of liverworts and lichens: clever, ancient organisms that have survived extinciton events and adapted to difficult conditions.

The polished stones present in the gemma cup were collected on the California coast. Many of the pieces in the body of work in this portfolio include these stones, sometimes hidden, and sometimes prominently displayed.
