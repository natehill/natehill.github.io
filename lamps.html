This page features boxes, bowls, lighting, and other items that I make, most of which are for sale upon request. If you are interested in my library and cultural heritage work, you can get into that over here.
All of these bowls are made from wood that I find locally or driving around my region in upstate New York. I’m particularly interested in spalted wood, wood that has begun to decay. The patterns, textures, and tones that fungi and other agents bring to the wood give it unique character. In the future, I‘ll be adding sculptural wooden boxes and other fixtures to the page as well.
Thanks for looking, and please reach out with any questions to nathanielhill@gmail.com or find me on Instagram as @natehillnatehill.

My stacked plywood lamps are only available in communities of 3, 5, 7, or other prime numbers. The lamps are meant to have a special, more private interior while the exterior presence evokes an architectural envelope, carapace, or a membrane. When lit, the interior spaces connect with one another, opening an unlikely bridge between one organism to another.

The lamps pictured here have all settled into comfortable ecosystems in New York, Florida, or California. They all look nice outside, but they aren’t weatherproof so I’d encourage anyone interested to try to raise a community of their own to place them in a south facing room with moderate to strong light. Their ability to network with one another or with other communities of lamps remains mysterious and undocumented, so please exercise caution.

Drop me a line if you want to try to raise some of your own.
